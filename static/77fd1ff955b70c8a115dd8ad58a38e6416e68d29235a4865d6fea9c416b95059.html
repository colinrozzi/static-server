What is the Tour de France?

Introduction

The Tour de France is an annual men's multiple stage bicycle race primarily held in France, while also occasionally passing through nearby countries. It is considered one of the most difficult and prestigious cycling races in the world.

History

The first Tour de France was held in 1903 to increase sales for the newspaper L'Auto. The race has been held annually ever since, except for during the two World Wars.

Race Structure

The Tour de France usually consists of 21 day-long stages over a 23-day period and covers approximately 3,500 kilometers. The race includes a mix of flat stages, mountain stages, and individual time trials.

Jerseys

During the race, different colored jerseys are awarded for various achievements:

  • Yellow Jersey: Worn by the overall time leader.
  • Green Jersey: Awarded to the leader in the points classification.
  • Polka-Dot Jersey: Worn by the best climber (mountain stages).
  • White Jersey: Given to the best young rider (under-26).
