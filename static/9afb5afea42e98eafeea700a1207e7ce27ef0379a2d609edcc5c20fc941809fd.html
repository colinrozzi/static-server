What is the Tour de France?

Introduction

The Tour de France is one of the most prestigious and well-known cycling races in the world. It is an annual men's multiple stage bicycle race primarily held in France, while also occasionally passing through nearby countries.

History

The Tour de France was first organized in 1903 to increase sales for the newspaper L'Auto. Since then, it has been held annually, except for breaks during the two World Wars. It has grown in popularity and now attracts professional cyclists from all over the globe.

Race Structure

The race typically covers around 3,500 kilometers (2,200 mi) and lasts for three weeks in July. It consists of 21 day-long stages over the course of 23 days. These stages include flat stages, hilly stages, and mountain stages. There are also time trials where riders race against the clock.
