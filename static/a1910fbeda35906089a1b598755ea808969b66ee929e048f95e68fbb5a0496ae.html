Introduction to the Tour de France

What is the Tour de France?

The Tour de France is an annual men's multiple stage bicycle race primarily held in France, while also occasionally passing through nearby countries.

History

The race was first organized in 1903 to increase sales for the newspaper L'Auto; it is currently run by the Amaury Sport Organisation.

How It Works

The race lasts for 23 days and it includes 21 day-long segments (stages) over a 3,500 km route. The cyclist with the shortest total time over the course of all the stages wins.

There are different types of stages:

  • Flat stages
  • Mountain stages
  • Time trials
